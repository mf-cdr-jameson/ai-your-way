# Technique cards

Standalone, copy-pasteable techniques for working with AI when focus, starting, or finishing is hard. **No tool required** — they work in Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT, or on paper. Each card is a tiny pattern you can use or paste into a prompt.

Designed ADHD-first, but useful to lots of people. Use the ones that help; ignore the rest.

---

## 1. Smallest first step
**For:** can't get started.
The enemy is the size of step one. Shrink it until it's almost silly.
- Ask the AI: *"Give me the smallest possible first step for [task] — something I can do in 2 minutes with no prep."*
- Do only that. Starting is the win; momentum does the rest.

## 2. TL;DR-first
**For:** walls of text are exhausting.
- Add to any prompt: *"Answer in one line first, then details underneath I can read only if I want."*
- Lets you act on the headline and dig deeper *only* if you choose.

## 3. One thing at a time
**For:** menus of options cause decision paralysis.
- Ask: *"Don't give me a list of options — recommend one default and tell me the single next action."*
- You can always ask for alternatives after.

## 4. Externalise the plan + done-list
**For:** losing the thread, forgetting what's done.
- Ask the AI to keep a running list: **Done · Doing now · Next.**
- Say *"update the list"* after each step, and *"where was I?"* whenever you return. Your working memory lives in the chat, not your head.

## 5. Parking lot
**For:** tangents and shiny new ideas pulling you off task.
- Tell the AI: *"If I go off on a tangent, capture it under a 'Parking Lot' heading and steer me back."*
- Nothing's lost, so it's safe to stay focused.

## 6. Time-box + break nudge
**For:** hyperfocus, or tasks that sprawl.
- Ask: *"Suggest a realistic time-box for this, and remind me to take a break / check scope when it's up."*
- Protects against both under- and over-doing it.

## 7. Scope guardrail
**For:** scope creep during hyperfocus.
- State the goal up front: *"The only goal today is X. If we drift beyond that, flag it and ask before continuing."*

## 8. Define "good enough" first
**For:** perfectionism, never finishing.
- Before starting: *"Help me define what 'done and good enough' looks like for this, in 3 bullets, before we begin."*
- Then you're aiming at a line, not infinity.

## 9. Neutral feedback / RSD-aware
**For:** rejection-sensitivity, shame spirals.
- Ask: *"Give feedback as neutral, specific, fixable points — no praise sandwich, no judgement."*
- Mistakes become tasks, not verdicts.

## 10. Celebrate the small win
**For:** motivation, finishing.
- When something's done, say so and let the AI mark it complete on the done-list. Acknowledging progress is fuel, not fluff.

## 11. "Where was I?" recap
**For:** coming back after an interruption or a new day.
- Just ask: *"Recap in 3 lines: what's done, what we're doing now, what's next."*
